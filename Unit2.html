Unit 2 : Exploring Two Variable Data

Important Concepts

  • Response Varaible - Measures the outcome of the study
  • Explanatory Variable - May predict or explain changes in the response variable
  • Scatterplot : Shows the relationship between two quantitative variables on the same dataset by plotting each individual data point as a point on the graph with one variable on one axis on the other variable on the other axis
    • Useful to plot an overall rough shape
  • How to Describe a ScatterPlot?
    • Direction
      • Positive
      • Negative
      • None
    • Form - Shape of the graph
      • Linear
      • None Linear
      • Quadratic
    • Strength - How much the graph Deviates from its form
    • Unusual Features
      • Clusters
      • Outliers
  • Correlation coefficient r
    • The sign of r determiners the direction of the graph and the value determines the association. (closer |r| is to 1, the stronger)
    • Note: Correlation is not causation

Calculator Functions

  • Regression Lines : Models how y (response variable) is predicted to change over changes in x (explanatory variable) Usually in the form y = a + bx
    • Slope b - amount of units y is predicted to change over a change of 1 unit of x
    • Usually (always if problem-makers donâ€™t say otherwise), to calculate a regression line from a scatter plot, least squares regression line is used to minimize the sum of squares of the vertical distances between data points and the line
  • Coefficient of Determination r^2 measures percentage of the variation in the response variable is due to the variation of the explanatory variable
  • Influential data points can greatly affect correlation and regression calculations
    • Points with x very far away from the average x are said to have high leverage and can be very influential
    • Points with large residuals are called outliers and can also affect calculations greatly
  • Relative Frequencies
    • Marginal relative frequency gives the percent of individuals that have a specific value for one categorical variable
    • Joint relative frequency gives the percent of individuals that have specific values for both categorical variables
    • Conditional relative frequency gives the percent of individuals that have a specific value for one categorical variable based on the fact that they have a specific value for the other categorical variable
